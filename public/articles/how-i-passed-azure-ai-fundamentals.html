How I passed Microsoft Azure AI Fundamentals AI-900

June 26, 2026

Study plan

I first came across the AI-900 after seeing someone mention it online. After a bit of browsing on the Microsoft Learn platform, I could not escape the AI-900 learning path. The test cost $99 and phased out at the end of the month, and is being replaced by what looks like a harder version, the AI-901. What really caught my eye was that this certification does not expire.

I blazed through a few learning modules and with 2 weeks left before the exam change, I impulsively purchased a voucher. At this point I was locked in, and I committed to studying about an hour each afternoon. I started with the Microsoft Learn AI-900 learning path, and purchased Nikolai Schuler's AI-900 Udemy course (Udemy folks know the drill, always wait for a sale).

I completed the Microsoft learning path, and then with a few days left prior to testing, I watched John Savill's AI-900 Study Cram v2 on YouTube to really solidify my understanding of a few key concepts. John Savill does offer a Generative AI Study Cram, but I didn't feel the need to watch it.

One thing of note is that the Microsoft training material was outdated. I know that won't matter with the new test releasing, but I was surprised to see that the Microsoft Learn, Udemy, etc. were referencing outdated or totally obsolete services. In one case, the service had been deprecated, and then replaced twice. Digging through Azure I was eventually able to find everything I needed, but the Azure UI is not intuitive at all.

Was the Exam Worth it?

Artificial Intelligence is still in its infancy, and there aren't many entry level, vendor neutral AI certifications available. The closest thing I found were CompTIA's compcerts, which are just trainings with a certificate of completion rather than a certification. For now, the best entry level AI certifications are all vendor specific, with Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Nvidia, and IBM all offering strong programs in their respective ecosystems.

That being said, the AI-900 wasn't entirely Microsoft specific. Roughly half of the exam covered Azure specific services, and the other half focused on general AI concepts, terminology, and use cases.

I would give the AI-900 a 7/10 for value and a 2/10 for difficulty. The cert is relevant for anyone looking to establish themselves in AI, and the vendor backing certainly carries some weight. The exam is entry level, and the concepts are approachable even for those with no technical background.

Parting Thoughts

With the new AI-901 releasing in the coming days, one thing is already clear from the learning material: the new test is a technical step up. Unlike the AI-900, AI-901 leans heavily on Python programming, with test takers expected to work directly with the SDKs. I'm not sure if the AI-901 will be a better certification than the AI-900, but I do know that it will be a more technically difficult exam.

How I passed CompTIA Network+ N10-009

October 26, 2025
CertNetworking

Study plan

I have a strong networking background, but when I started Net+ I think I tried to study too many things at once. This cert is a mile wide and an inch deep. Eventually, I was able to establish a plan that really worked for me. I locked into a 30-day schedule studying no more than 3 hours a day and based everything on the official N10-009 objectives. Before jumping into videos or practice tests, I assessed each of the domains so I knew where to spend my time. This kept my study sessions focused and prevented over-studying topics that weren't going to be on the exam.

I didn't jump into multiple study sources at once. I first completed Professor Messer's Youtube series to build baseline understanding across all domains. Then, I used Jason Dion's Udemy course specifically for reinforcement on topics I didn't understand or needed help memorizing like cabling standards, connector types, distinguishing wireless protocols, etc. I very much treated Jason Dion's content as supplemental and didn't go through the whole course again, only sections I didn't fully understand.

In my final study phase, I switched to practice exams. I don't think I did anything special here... I reviewed missed questions, if I didn't know something I googled it. The last thing I did that I recommend to everyone is to review the objectives one at a time. If you can explain each objective clearly, you're ready to pass. Also, if you buy paid material, wait for a sale.

Was the Exam Worth it?

I think it depends where you are in your career. This is a great vendor neutral cert that teaches you a lot about a lot. It touches topics that the CCNA won't, but also doesn't go as deep into networking as the CCNA. If you're looking to get into networking, IT, or cybersecurity, this is a great starting point. If you already have a strong networking background, this may not be worth your time and money unless you need it to satisfy a job requirement. I'm giving the Net+ a 7/10 for value.

Exam tips & Parting Thoughts

Review what you can the morning of your exam, and data dump what you need on the provided dry erase board. Skip over and save the PBQs for last, read questions carefully, and flag any questions you're unsure of to return to later. Network+ N10-009 is a very straightforward exam for those who come prepared. Good luck!

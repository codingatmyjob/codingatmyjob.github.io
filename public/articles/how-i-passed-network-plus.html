How I passed CompTIA Network+

October 26, 2025
CERTNetworking

Study plan

Let me start by saying I have a very strong networking background with almost 4 years on cisco networking equipment, so I didn't think this was going to be a very challenging exam. I basically chalked it up to a resume filler. It took me 30 days and I used 4 sources to study. I started by watching Professor Messer's Network+ YouTube series to gauge my overall level of preparedness. I then purchased Jason Dion's Network+ Udemy course that came with a free practice exam to get a second teaching style for the areas I was struggling with (mainly cables, connector types, and wireless standards - all the frustrating memorization). At this point I could explain most of the exam objectives pretty well. If you plan on purchasing a Udemy course, wait until there's a sale on the course or you'll spend way more money than you need to.

Resources

  • Official exam objectives — If you can explain each objective, you're ready to pass - FREE
  • Professor Messer Network+ YouTube series — FREE
  • Jason Dion Network+ (N10-009) Full Course & Practice Exam — $16.99
  • Make sure your study material is approved by CompTIA

Was the Exam Worth it?

I think it depends where you are in your career. This is a great vendor neutral cert that teaches you a lot about networks and network protocols. If you're looking to get into networking, IT, or cybersecurity, this is a great starting point. If you already have a strong networking background, this may not be worth your time and money unless you need it for a job requirement. I'm giving the Net+ a 6.5/10 for value.

Exam tips & Parting Thoughts

Review what you can the morning of your exam, and data dump what you need on the provided dry erase board. Skip over and save the PBQs for last, read questions carefully, and flag any questions you're unsure of to return to later. Network+ N10-009 is a very straightforward exam for those who come prepared. Good luck!

How I passed CompTIA Security+ SY0-701

October 26, 2025
CertSecurity

Study plan

Fresh off finishing the Net+, I treated Security+ like a sprint and kept to my routine: I gave myself 5 weeks and spent about 2 to 4 hours studying on weeknights and had longer study sessions on weekends. Early on, I made sure everything I studied fit the SY0-701 objectives so I wouldn't waste time.

I didn't try to juggle too many resources at once. I kept what works for me, and started with Professor Messer's Security+ YouTube series to get a solid foundation. I then used Jason Dion's content as reinforcement for topics I needed to revisit. The biggest thing I focused on was vocabulary and acronyms, since this exam is loaded with them and they show up in almost every domain. I don't think I can stress the acronyms enough, knowing them (even without fully understanding the underlying content - just breaking out the acronym) easily earned me 3+ questions on the exam.

In my final study leg, I switched to practice exams. I used Sybex practice exams (an actual book) to study at work, and alternated between those and the Jason Dion practice exams. If I missed a question, I reviewed the correlating objective until I could explain it clearly in plain language. I repeated that until I was satisfied with my scores. If you buy paid material, wait for a sale.

Was the Exam Worth it?

Absolutely. Security+ is a great cybersecurity certification that provides a solid foundation in key security concepts, making it a great starting point. It also satisfies DoD 8570/8140 requirements, which is a major requirement for many government cybersecurity jobs. I’d give the value of Security+ a 9/10 for anyone looking to establish themselves in cybersecurity.

Study & Exam day tips

Learn the acronyms! Security+ is the most acronym‑heavy exam I’ve taken, with well over 250 terms (some duplicates too so you need to know context). Focus your extended study on the objectives where your practice scores are weakest. During the exam, skip PBQs and return to them later; pace yourself through the multiple‑choice section, flag any questions you're unsure of, and revisit them before submitting.

Final thoughts

Study whatever way works best for you, and check out other people's study methods on Youtube, Reddit, etc. Good luck!

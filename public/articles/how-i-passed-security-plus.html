How I passed CompTIA Security+ SY0-701

October 26, 2025 CERTSecurity

Study plan

I had roughly five weeks between finishing Net+ and my scheduled Security+ exam. I studied consistently — about 2–4 hours most weeknights and 4–6 hours on weekends — focusing solely on Security+. I started with Jason Dion’s Udemy course to build a solid foundation, then moved on to Professor Messer’s YouTube series to reinforce and touch up on any topics that I still didn't fully understand. After about two and a half weeks of coursework I shifted to practice exams: Jason Dion’s tests to identify weak objectives, and Sybex’s sectional tests to drill each domain. By exam day I could confidently explain each of the objectives and felt prepared. In the resources listed below I've included all my study sources, and the listed price I bought them for. Let me say this: If you've never used Udemy before, sign up, and wait for a sale. Check the course pricing on Udemy daily, there's basically a sale every week.

Was the Exam Worth it?

Absolutely. Security+ is a great entry-level IT & cybersecurity certification that provides a solid foundation in security concepts, making it a great starting point for those new to the field. It also satisfies DoD 8570/8140 requirements, which is a major requirement for many government roles. I’d give the value of Security+ a 9/10 for anyone looking to establish themselves in cybersecurity.

Study & Exam day tips

Learn the acronyms! Security+ is the most acronym‑heavy exam I’ve taken, with well over 200 terms (some even overlap). Focus your extended study on the objectives where your practice scores are weakest. Before the exam, get a good night’s sleep and a light study session in the morning. During the exam, skip PBQs and return to them later; pace yourself through the multiple‑choice section, flag any questions you're unsure of, and revisit them before submitting.

Final thoughts

Study whatever way works best for you, and check out study guides on Youtube, Udemy, Reddit, etc. Good luck.

How I passed CompTIA Network+

October 26, 2025 Certification

Short version: focused fundamentals, lots of packet-level labs, structured practice tests, and scheduled review.

Study plan

Break the objectives into networking domains (OSI model, IP addressing/routing, switching, wireless, security). I used 1–2 week blocks per major domain with hands-on labs and flashcards for memorization.

Resources

  • Official exam objectives — use as a checklist.
  • Video course plus a concise Network+ book for reference.
  • Packet-tracing tools (Wireshark) and small VM/network lab for practice.
  • Question banks to simulate exam timing and to identify weak areas.

Hands-on labs

Set up basic topologies to practice subnetting, routing, VLANs, ACLs, and wireless configuration. Inspect traffic with Wireshark — understanding packets made theoretical questions trivial.

Exam day tips

Rest well beforehand, bring a printed formula cheat-sheet for quick review (where allowed), read multi-step scenarios carefully, and eliminate impossible answers first.

Final thoughts

Consistent hands-on practice combined with targeted question review made the biggest difference. Good luck.

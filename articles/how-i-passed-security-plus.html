How I passed CompTIA Security+ SY0-701

October 26, 2025 Certification

Short version: structured study, lots of practice questions, and put in the hours.

Study plan

I had roughly five weeks between finishing Net+ and my scheduled Security+ exam. I studied consistently — about 2–4 hours most weeknights and 4–6 hours on weekends — focusing solely on Security+. I started with Jason Dion’s Udemy course to build a solid foundation, then moved on to Professor Messer’s YouTube series to reinforce and touch up on any topics that I still didn't fully understand. After about two and a half weeks of coursework I shifted to practice exams: Jason Dion’s tests to identify weak objectives, and Sybex’s sectional tests to drill each domain. By exam day I could confidently explain each of the objectives and felt prepared. In the resources listed below I've included all my study sources, and the listed price I bought them for. Study & Exam day tips

Learn the acronyms! Security+ is the most acronym‑heavy exam I’ve taken, with well over 200 terms (some even overlap). Focus your extended study on the objectives where your practice scores are weakest. Before the exam, get a good night’s sleep and a light study session in the morning. In the test, skip performance‑based questions at first and return to them later; pace yourself through the multiple‑choice section, flag any questions you're unsure of, and revisit them before submitting.

Final thoughts

Study whatever way works best for you, and check out study guides on Youtube, Udemy, Reddit, etc. Good luck.

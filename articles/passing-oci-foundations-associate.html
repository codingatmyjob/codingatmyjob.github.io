Passing Oracle's OCI Foundations Associate

May 15, 2026
CertCloud

Study plan

I was bummed out in the fall when I realized I missed Oracle's 'Race to Certification 2025'. I thought after the event ended, certifications would return to regular pricing. Over 6 months later, I was surprised to see that not only are some of the exams free, but the corresponding learning paths are too! The intro to Oracle cert had a pending retirement date of 29 May, 2026 which was recently extended to 15 June, 2026. With the exam being free, the learning path being free, and a hard deadline to meet, I started the OCI Foundations Associate 2025 learning path.

The learning path takes 4 hours, and has 8 distinct sections (only 6 of which have any substantive content). Those are IAM, Networking, Compute, Storage, Security, and Governance and Administration. Upon completing the learning path, there is an optional but encouraged 2 hour hands-on lab. For the record I skipped the hands on lab and did just fine on the exam. Everything you need is in the learning path.

For studying, I really zeroed in on the video trainings (not the demos), and took detailed notes on each section, and the core concepts that stood out to me. I tried to limit myself to around an hour to an hour a half each day, and was able to fully complete the learning path and exam in under a week!

Was the Course and Certification Worth it

If you're looking for an introductory course to cloud computing, or want a glimpse into Oracle's ecosystem, this is a great course, and Oracle did a great job putting together the video-based trainings. The added visual aids are very helpful for the more challenging concepts like networking, security, and storage.

The online exam process was very easy. They give step by step instructions, and then you can begin the exam. It's an hour long 40 question test with a 65% passing score, allowing you to miss 14 questions. They also allow 15 free test attempts... so take that for what it's worth. If you complete the learning path, you should have no problem passing the exam.

It's a very entry level certification, and outside of Oracle I don't think it will hold much weight with employers, especially compared to similar cloud certifications like AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner or Microsoft Azure Fundamentals. I think this course is a great intro to cloud, and I will be taking another certification from Oracle, but I wouldn't put much stock value in this particular certification.
